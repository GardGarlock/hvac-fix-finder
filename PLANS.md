## Overview

We help electrical contractors recover revenue by handling phone-related customer communication that usually falls through the cracks: electrical customer texts, stale electrical lead callbacks, and inbound calls.

The offer is not a broad marketing automation package. It is a focused phone operations layer that helps an electrical business respond faster, keep conversations moving, and protect owners, CSRs, dispatchers, and field staff from call overload.

## The Problem

Most electrical contractors lose opportunities because:

- Text conversations are scattered across phones, inboxes, and staff members.
- Old electrical leads are not called back with a clear, respectful cadence.
- Incoming calls arrive while staff are on job sites, driving, serving customers, or closed for the day.
- Voicemails and missed calls create delay at the exact moment the customer wants help.

## The Solution

We install and operate a simple phone support layer around the electrical business.

The system is designed to:

- Reply to and organize customer texts.
- Call back stale leads from prior electrical service inquiries, estimates, or incomplete bookings.
- Pick up and handle incoming calls using approved scripts, routing rules, and escalation instructions.
- Give the business clean summaries of conversations, lead status, and next actions.

## The Core Offer

### 1. Text Handling

We help manage electrical customer text conversations so customers are not left waiting or buried in a staff member's phone.

Included workflows:

- **Customer text response:** Reply to new and active customer texts using approved tone, answers, and next-step rules.
- **Scheduling and intake prompts:** Collect details such as service type, urgency, location, access notes, and preferred appointment windows.
- **Conversation summaries:** Keep the business informed on who reached out, what they need, and what should happen next.

**Goal:** Keep text conversations moving without relying on the owner, dispatcher, or field staff to monitor every message.

### 2. Stale Lead Callbacks

We call back older electrical leads that already showed interest but never booked, answered, or made a final decision.

Included workflows:

- **Lead list cleanup:** Segment past inquiries by source, age, service type, and last known status when data is available.
- **Respectful callback cadence:** Use concise phone scripts that reference the prior inquiry and offer a clear next step.
- **Disposition tracking:** Mark outcomes such as booked, not interested, bad number, needs follow-up, or already solved.

**Goal:** Recover useful conversations from leads the business already paid to generate, without aggressive or spammy outreach.

### 3. Incoming Call Handling

We pick up and handle incoming electrical business calls so customers reach a person or a defined process instead of a dead end.

Included workflows:

- **Live intake:** Answer calls, gather caller details, service needs, urgency, location, panel/generator/EV charger context when relevant, and preferred appointment windows.
- **Routing and escalation:** Transfer, notify, or escalate urgent calls according to the business's rules.
- **Call notes and handoff:** Send structured call summaries so staff know what happened and what action is required.

**Goal:** Increase call coverage, reduce voicemail dependency, and make every caller easier for the team to act on.

## Platform & Delivery

The system can sit on top of the electrical contractor's existing phone, CRM, calendar, and dispatch process. We should not promise a specific integration unless it has been verified for that client.

### Implementation Inputs

We typically need:

- Business hours and after-hours rules.
- Service area and electrical service categories.
- Intake questions and disqualification rules.
- Escalation contacts and emergency handling instructions.
- Approved text and call scripts.
- Access to the phone/text system, CRM, spreadsheet, or lead list needed to perform the work.

### Compliance Guardrails

The service should:

- Use honest caller/text identification.
- Honor stop, unsubscribe, and do-not-contact requests.
- Avoid promotional mass texting without appropriate consent.
- Keep outreach tied to the customer's prior inquiry or active service relationship.
- Treat compliance as a client-specific legal and operational requirement, not as a guaranteed outcome.
