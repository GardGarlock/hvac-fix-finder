Representative Deliverable
Situation Assessment

What I Found in Week One

The seller ran dispatch from memory and a whiteboard. Customer follow-up was ad hoc — maybe 20% of unsold quotes ever got a second touch. Technicians wrote job notes in ServiceTitan sporadically. No system for converting one-time customers to service agreement holders. Revenue was healthy but the operation was entirely in the seller’s head. Classic owner-dependent business, and classic opportunity.

Priority Quick Wins

Four AI implementations. All live within 30 days.

Quick Win 01

Quote Follow-Up System

Claude drafts personalized follow-up emails and texts for every open quote older than 48 hours. CSR reviews and sends in under 2 minutes. Previously, 80% of quotes got no follow-up.

+15–25% close rate on open quotes
Quick Win 02

Technician Job Note Completion

Claude turns a 2-sentence voice memo from a tech into a complete ServiceTitan job note, including next recommended services. Reduces admin time, improves data quality, flags upsell opportunities.

40 min/day admin saved per tech
Quick Win 03

Service Agreement Upsell Identification

Each week Claude scans the customer list and identifies accounts that have had 2+ visits without a service agreement. Produces a prioritized call list with a suggested pitch for each account type.

Recurring revenue conversion engine
Quick Win 04

Seller Knowledge Capture

Two-hour structured interview with the departing seller. Claude processes the transcript and produces vendor relationships, customer quirks, pricing logic, and informal processes — documented permanently.

Tribal knowledge made transferable
Implementation Timeline

90 Days from First Engagement

Week 1–2
  • Discovery: observe dispatch, ride-alongs with 2 techs, pull 90 days of job data
  • Seller knowledge capture interview — full documentation
  • Audit ServiceTitan data quality and workflow gaps
Week 3–5
  • Quote follow-up system live — prompts built, CSR trained, first sends
  • Job note voice-to-text workflow deployed to all techs
  • First service agreement upsell list produced and worked
Week 6–8
  • Customer communication templates live (estimates, completion, reviews)
  • Weekly AI output review — calibrate prompts based on CSR feedback
  • Seasonal demand planning: build AI-assisted marketing calendar for shoulder season
Day 60–90
  • Measure: close rate on quotes, service agreement conversion, admin hours saved
  • Document all AI workflows in the operating manual
  • Train successor CSR or office manager to own and maintain the system
Example Prompt (Quote Follow-Up)

What the system actually looks like in practice

Prompt deployed in Claude — CSR uses daily
You are a customer service assistant for [Company Name], a residential HVAC company in [City]. We are following up on an open estimate. Customer: {customer_name} Service quoted: {service_description} Quote amount: {quote_amount} Days since quote: {days_open} Last contact note: {last_note} Write a warm, non-pushy follow-up message (email format). Acknowledge that decisions take time. Remind them of the value. Offer to answer questions or adjust scope. Match our tone: honest, local, not salesy. Keep it under 120 words. Use their first name.
