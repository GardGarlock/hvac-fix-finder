What multiple would your business command if you sold today?

Nine questions. Two minutes. See your estimated EBITDA multiple, your top operational gaps, and exactly what’s suppressing the value of what you own.

0 of 9 answered
1 of 9  ·  Financial Clarity

What is your annual EBITDA?

Earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization — your true business profit. Larger businesses trade at higher multiples and attract more buyers.

2 of 9  ·  Sales Independence

What is your revenue trend over the last 2 years?

Buyers pay premiums for growth. Declining revenue significantly suppresses multiples.

3 of 9  ·  Team Redundancy

How many hours per week are you personally required in the business?

Owner dependency is the #1 multiple suppressor. Buyers won't pay a premium for a job.

4 of 9  ·  Financial Clarity

How would you describe your financial records?

Clean books require COGS properly separated, labor correctly allocated, and gross profit clearly visible.

5 of 9  ·  Digital Infrastructure

Do you have a CRM or field service software in consistent daily use?

Buyers need to see the full customer base. If it lives in a spreadsheet, it doesn't transfer at closing.

6 of 9  ·  Team Redundancy

Do you have written SOPs for your core workflows?

Knowledge trapped in one person's head is a liability at closing. Buyers can't replicate it. Lenders can't underwrite it.

7 of 9  ·  Repeatable Revenue

What percentage of your revenue is recurring (contracts, service agreements)?

Recurring revenue is the most valuable revenue. SBA lenders fund it. Buyers pay premiums for predictable income.

8 of 9  ·  Repeatable Revenue

What is your largest single customer as a percentage of revenue?

High customer concentration is a significant red flag in diligence and can collapse deals at the bank.

9 of 9  ·  Sales Independence

Does your business generate new revenue without you personally involved?

If growth depends on your relationships, it's not a transferable asset — it's a job.
